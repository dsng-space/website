// Program Timeline

Five milestones to first hot-fire

Reactor Core
01 — 2025 Q3

Reactor Core Concept

NERVA-class graphite composite at 25 kN rated thrust. Detailed neutronics analysis and fuel element geometry locked.

Test Article
02 — 2026 Q1

Subscale Test Article

Cold-flow hydrogen loop, full instrumentation suite, MARS-equivalent vacuum chamber qualification.

Hot-Fire Stand
03 — 2027 Q2

First Hot-Fire

Open-cycle hydrogen propellant, 10-second steady-state burn at 50% rated thrust. Full telemetry capture.

Engine Qual
04 — 2028

Flight Engine Qualification

Full-duration burns across thermal cycling envelope. Multi-restart demonstrated. DoE radiological approval completed.

Cislunar Demo
05 — 2030

Cislunar Demonstration

Earth–Moon transit, engine restart in lunar orbit, return burn. First NTP flight demonstration since NERVA.

// Mission

We are building nuclear thermal propulsion for the next generation of crewed and robotic missions — engines that cut transit times to Mars in half and open the rest of the solar system to fast, capable spacecraft.
